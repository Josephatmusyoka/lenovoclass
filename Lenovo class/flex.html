A key component of retail operations is inventory management, which forms the basis for effective customer service and long-term profitability. The complexities of inventory management are particularly noticeable in the setting of tiny boutique firms like Umoja Collection, located in Kutus, Kirinyaga County. In order to gain knowledge from previous research and inform the creation of the Umoja Collection Inventory Management System, this part examines significant literature in the field of inventory management.
Small boutique companies, like Umoja Collection, cater to customers of all ages and genders with their varied product offerings, which include headwear, top wear, bottom wear, footwear, beddings, briefcases, belts, and undergarments. Contrary to larger businesses, boutiques frequently struggle with little resources, making careful inventory management crucial to their operations.
For retail customers, Umoja Collection specifically uses a dynamic pricing strategy. Retail prices are individualized based on agreements reached between clients and shop employees. This unique pricing strategy makes the inventory management process more complex, so a system that can easily adjust to different price structures is required.
